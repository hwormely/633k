The field

Competitor profiles

YouVersion — The Bible App

1B+ installs · free

The category's gravity well and the price floor — fully free, ministry-funded, with unmatched translation breadth (2,500+ versions), thousands of plans, and strong social. Thin on deep original-language tooling and real AI study (AI is limited to recommendations); light maps/timeline. Impossible to beat on reach or price.

Logos (Faithlife)

Subscription · scholar-grade

The serious-study incumbent and the strongest depth competitor: deepest original-language stack, pro Atlas, Factbook, timeline, and a real AI Study Assistant over your library. But expensive (Premium/Pro/Max), complex, and desktop-bred — dense on mobile. Built for pastors and academics, not seekers.

Olive Tree (HarperCollins)

Freemium · publisher-backed

A polished study app, freshly redesigned (v8.0, late 2025) with AI "Smart Search." Solid original-language Bibles, offline, maps, commentaries. No community layer; AI is search, not synthesis; monetization leans on à-la-carte resource purchases.

Blue Letter Bible

Free · depth-for-free

The best free original-language tool — powerful interlinear, BDAG-level lexicons, TSK cross-references (50+/verse). The closest analog to 633k's interlinear ambition, but trapped in a dated, clunky interface with no AI, community, or maps. The exact gap a sleek AI-native app exploits.

Dwell

~$60/yr · audio-first

The category-defining audio Bible: 20+ narrators, original music, sleep mode, memorization. Best-in-class listening — but single-purpose. No original languages, cross-references, or AI study. Competes with 633k only on audio, where human narration beats AI-TTS.

Hallow

22M+ downloads · ~$70/yr

The breakout faith-app success (Catholic-focused): 10,000+ guided sessions, real AI (Hallow AI), strong community, proven willingness-to-pay. Not a Bible-study app — no interlinear, cross-references, or deep Scripture engagement. The clearest proof a polished, AI-equipped, paid faith app can reach mass scale.

Bible Gateway

Freemium · web-first

The dominant Bible website with a competent app: 200+ translations free, Plus (~$35–50/yr) unlocks study libraries and ad-free audio. No meaningful AI or community; the mobile experience lives in the website's shadow.

Glorify

25M+ downloads · freemium

A habit-forming daily-worship app: devotionals, meditations, worship, streaks, community annotations, Bible games — plus a 2025 move into hardware (the Glorify Ring). Scales on daily habit but shallow on actual study. Competes for devotional minutes, not deep-study users.

Scale figures (Hallow 22M+, Glorify 25M+, YouVersion 1B+) are company-reported / store-listed, not independently audited.
So what

Synthesis

Where 633k wins

  • AI depth, fused. Doctrinally-reviewed tap-verse AI + original languages + cross-refs + modern design — in one app. No one else occupies this quadrant.
  • The Connections engine. A personal knowledge-graph of Scripture (every entity a node, semantic + lexical). Genuinely novel; most defensible.
  • Doctrinal review gate. A trust layer for AI output no competitor advertises.
  • Experiential unity. Timeline + maps + journeys + study + community, unified.

At parity

  • Reading plans, journaling, notes, highlights, offline reading.
  • Audio Bible — at/below parity vs Dwell's human narration.
  • Original languages — par with Blue Letter & Olive Tree; behind Logos.
  • Community — conceptually competitive, but unproven at launch.

Gaps to watch

  • Translation breadth. Public-domain-first (WEB/KJV/ASV) vs NIV/ESV/NLT users expect. The most concrete near-term gap.
  • Scale & network effects. YouVersion/Hallow/Glorify have flywheels a launch can't match.
  • Scholarship. Logos still far ahead for academics.
  • Free floor. YouVersion & Blue Letter are free and rich.

The positioning wedge

Lead with the Connections engine and tap-to-context AI — the things no one else has. Not translation count or audio, where incumbents win.
