633k is a Seeker's Kingdom Operating System — an AI-native Bible study and faith-community app built on one verse: seek first the kingdom of God. This brief walks every screen: what it's for, why it serves the Seeker, the words on it, and where it leads.

The system

One language across every surface.

A Fog-light canvas, a Midnight anchor, and a rotating palette of color-coded cards — some boldly filled, some Fog-white with an accent border. Huge tight headlines, scripture set in a literary serif, and first-class motion: everything rises, springs, and counts up. One job per screen — except Discover, where content is meant to flood.

The thread that ties it

Everything is a node.

Verses, notes, entries, projects, prayers, thinkers, places — every object in 633k is a node in one Connections engine. That's why a verse in the Reader, a place on the map, and a line in your journal can all find each other. It's the quiet structure beneath every screen, and the one thing no other Bible app has.
